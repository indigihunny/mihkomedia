Four Medicines

Acrylic | Four canvases | 16" by 24"

Growing up, Kaitlyn was taught about the four sacred medicines: Cedar, Tobacco, Sage, and Sweetgrass. Her mother would prepare cedar water, valued for its medicinal properties. She learned that tobacco is offered to the land and to the Creator, both as a way to send prayers and to give thanks for the gifts harvested from the earth. Sweetgrass braids were often present throughout the home, and sage was regularly used for smudging.In her artwork, "the Four Medicines" are painted primarily in shades and tones of teal, accented with touches of redâ€”colors Kaitlyn is naturally drawn to in her creative practice. Each medicine is placed with intention, aligned with its traditional direction, and together the four canvases form one harmonious piece.

Indigenous Savage

Water Colour

"Indigenous Savage" is a self-portrait painted using watercolour. It was an experimental piece and showcased in the Ivviulutit art show in 2021 at the Aqsaarniit Hotel. Kaitlyn showcased her painting in a short Northbeat CBC interview and sold it the exhibition night, it resides in Iqaluit, Nunavut.
